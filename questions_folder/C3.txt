          <span class="solution-visible-txt mathjax-support">All submissions for this problem are available.</span><p class=" mathjax-support">In the magic land of Mathtopia, the words of the language are written only using two symbols: ones and zeros.
</p><p class=" mathjax-support">
A given word <var class=" mathjax-support">w</var> is called "prime" if it cannot be written in the form of the concatenation of several copies of some shorter word. So, for example the words '100', '1100', and '001100' are prime, while the words '0101', '100100', '1111', and '101010' are not prime.
</p><p class=" mathjax-support">Your task is to calculate the number of prime words which can be built from exactly <var class=" mathjax-support">a</var> ones and <var class=" mathjax-support">b</var> zeros.

</p><h3 class=" mathjax-support">Input</h3>
<p class=" mathjax-support">t - the number of test cases. For each test case, two integers: 1&lt;=<var class=" mathjax-support">a</var>&lt;=10<sup class=" mathjax-support">9</sup>, 1&lt;=<var class=" mathjax-support">b</var>&lt;=10<sup class=" mathjax-support">9</sup>.

</p><h3 class=" mathjax-support">Output</h3>
<p class=" mathjax-support">For each test case, output the required answer modulo 531169.

</p><h3 class=" mathjax-support">Example</h3>

<pre class=" mathjax-support"><b class=" mathjax-support">Input:</b>
1
2 2

<b class=" mathjax-support">Output:</b>
4
</pre>

Explanation: the 4 words from the example are:
'0011','1100','0110','1001'.
